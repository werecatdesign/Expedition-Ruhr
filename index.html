What is the Expedition Ruhr?

One weekend. Over 30 locations. A lifetime memory.

Expedition Ruhr is a 3-day puzzle game located in the German Ruhr Area. It takes place on a weekend in July and is organised in 3 days, the last of which is the finale. The game is played in competing teams and the winning team will receive a special prize!

Are you ready to explore and challenge yourself?

Your task with your team is to find a missing person by following hints and solving riddles in all different places across the area. But be prepared: Your target person is constantly moving and will not be easy to catch.

Be faster than the other teams and find the target person first! Master the challenges of the game and find out how strong you are as a team. On the final day of the game, a surprise awaits you...

What to Expect

A thrilling puzzle-game adventure that demands riddle-solving, quick thinking and teamwork. You and your team will explore an area that breathes the heritage of its past and hides mysteries and secret treasures alike.

Who We Are

Industry of the Ruhr Area

To most people, the Ruhr Area is primarily known for its history in coal mining and steel production. Mining was one of the central drivers of the industrialisation and economic growth in Germany. The mining industry also accounts for the urbanisation and geographical expansion of the Area, which includes cities like Cologne, Düsseldorf, Essen and many more.


Due to structural changes, mining lost some of its importance in the 20th century, leading to many mining facilities being shut down. Many of these can still be visited today. Besides mining, the economy of the Ruhr Area mainly relies on the services sector.

Nature of the Ruhr Area

Although known for its industry and cities, the Ruhr Area offers a lot of natural treasures. Even the name "Ruhr Area" has its origin in nature, as the area was named after its river Ruhr. In the area, there are 1,750 square kilometres of agricultural area and 800 square kilometres of forest, plus several lakes. Some of the forests have been left in their natural state for decades, offering untouched refuges for flora and fauna alike.


Throughout the history of mining, man has made his very own contributions to shaping nature. Stones that were by-products of coal mining were disposed of in nature, forming the so-called "Halden", artificial hills of several 100 metres of height. The by-products of mining and naturally occuring metals in the soil have created habitats for rare plants and insects.

Culture of the Ruhr Area

The culture of the Ruhr Area has majorly been shaped by mining. Working in a mine used to be a well-paid but rough job. Teamwork and communication were vital for the survival in the mines, and being a miner was a job to be proud of, since the mining industry was the backbone of the German economy. This history has shaped the area up until today. People of the Ruhr area are said to be very direct and honest, hard-working, communicative and social.


For visitors, the Ruhr Area offers a lot of cultural attractions. A variety of museums invite visitors to learn about the history, visit abandoned factory buildings or enjoy arts and music. The Ruhr Area has over 200 museums, 3,500 industrial monuments, and 250 festivals and celebrations. It is a very vibrant and living area with a variety of things to discover!
